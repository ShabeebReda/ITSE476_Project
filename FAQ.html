Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about Nexum collaborative documentation platform. If you can't find what you're looking for, feel free to contact our support team.

Getting Started

What is Nexum? +

Nexum is a free and open-source collaborative documentation platform that enables teams, developers, and educators to create and maintain project documentation together with real-time or version-controlled editing capabilities.

How do I get started with Nexum? +

Download the latest stable release from our Users page, install it on your system, and follow the quick start guide in our documentation. You can create your first collaborative document within minutes.

Is Nexum really free? +

Yes, Nexum is completely free and open-source under the MIT license. You can use it for personal, educational, or commercial purposes without any licensing fees.

Technical Questions

What platforms does Nexum support? +

Nexum supports Windows, macOS, and Linux distributions. We recently added Windows ARM support and expanded Linux compatibility. Mobile companion apps are currently in beta testing.

Can I use Nexum offline? +

Yes, Nexum works offline for individual document editing. For collaborative features, an internet connection is required to sync changes with team members in real-time.

How do I integrate Nexum with my existing workflow? +

Nexum offers APIs and integrations with popular development tools. You can export documents in various formats and integrate with version control systems like Git.

Collaboration Features

How many people can collaborate on a document? +

There's no hard limit on collaborators. Performance may vary based on your server configuration and network conditions, but most teams work comfortably with 10-50 simultaneous editors.

Does Nexum have version control? +

Yes, Nexum includes built-in version control with change tracking, revision history, and the ability to revert to previous versions. You can also integrate with external version control systems.

Can I control who has access to my documents? +

Absolutely. Nexum provides granular permission controls including view-only, edit, and admin access levels. You can invite specific users or create team-based access groups.

Troubleshooting

I'm having sync issues. What should I do? +

First, check your internet connection. If the problem persists, try refreshing the application or restarting. For persistent issues, check our GitHub issues page or contact support.

How do I report a bug? +

Visit our GitHub Issues page and create a new issue with detailed information about the problem, including your operating system, Nexum version, and steps to reproduce the bug.

Where can I get additional help? +

Join our community forum for peer support, check our comprehensive user manual, watch video tutorials, or contact our support team directly at support@nexum.org.
